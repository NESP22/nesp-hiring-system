# NESP Job-Ad Control Center

The **Job Ads** dashboard area prepares job-ad copy, tracked application links, campaign notes, renewal dates, and source reporting.

Sanitized callout screenshot: `docs/nesp/screenshots/job-ad-control-center-callouts.svg`

It does not:

- publish ads,
- spend money,
- create paid campaigns,
- set budgets,
- enter billing details,
- accept platform terms,
- create accounts,
- send applicant messages,
- rank candidates,
- reject candidates,
- or change candidate stages.

## Source Tracking

Use links in this format:

`https://careers.nesportsphoto.com/careers/?p=applyToJob&ID=[JOB_ID]&nesp_source=[SOURCE]`

Approved source codes:

- `nesp_website`
- `indeed`
- `facebook`
- `instagram`
- `linkedin`
- `craigslist`
- `college_board`
- `referral`
- `other`

When a candidate applies, the candidate source is stored as a readable label such as `NESP Ad: Indeed`.

## Dashboard Reporting

The dashboard reports:

- applications by source,
- applicants moved past initial review by a human,
- scheduled phone screens,
- completed phone screens,
- manual spend entered by Craig,
- cost per applicant.

Cost per applicant is only calculated from spend Craig enters manually. The system does not connect to ad billing systems.

## Recommended Rollout Order

1. NESP careers website links.
2. Free Indeed standard posts.
3. Free or low-cost college and photography school boards.
4. Google Business Profile post.
5. Local community groups where rules allow job posts.
6. Craigslist only after Craig approves each paid post.
7. Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn paid promotion only after Craig approves platform, budget, dates, and billing.

## Free Posting Opportunities

- NESP careers website.
- Indeed standard posts if available.
- College career boards.
- Photography schools and programs.
- Google Business Profile posts.
- Local community groups that allow job posts.

## Paid Options Requiring Approval

- Indeed sponsorship.
- Facebook boosts or Meta ads.
- Instagram paid promotion.
- LinkedIn paid jobs or promoted posts.
- Craigslist paid job postings.
- Any school board or niche board that charges a fee.

## What Craig Must Complete Manually

- Account login.
- Identity or business verification.
- Billing setup.
- Terms acceptance.
- Final preview approval.
- Publish button.
- Budget selection.
- Campaign activation.
- Applicant messages.

## What Codex Can Safely Prepare After Access Is Approved

- Draft copy.
- Tracked links.
- Image-size recommendations.
- Platform form field drafts.
- Campaign checklist.
- Preview review notes.
- Internal tracking setup.

Codex must still stop before publishing, payment, billing, account creation using Craig's identity, accepting terms, or messaging applicants.
