## Disclosures and Safety Checks

- Show the hourly range in both the Craigslist compensation field and the body. Massachusetts requires covered employers with 25 or more Massachusetts employees to disclose a good-faith pay range in job postings beginning October 29, 2025; showing the range for all three posts is the safer operating standard.
- State W-2 status for the field and staff-photographer roles. Do not describe W-2 benefits in the contractor post.
- State freelance/independent-contractor status prominently and keep that post blocked until the classification review is complete. A label, hourly rate, equipment ownership, or contractor agreement alone does not determine Massachusetts classification.
- State each role's listing season exactly. Do not add September-November or call these roles year-round.
- State territory, travel, early weekend hours, outdoor work, equipment expectations, and physical requirements only when they are genuinely job-related and accurate.
- Keep background-check language conditional and job-related. Do not request a Social Security number, date of birth, driver's-license scan, bank information, password, or other sensitive data in Craigslist copy or replies.
- Do not ask applicants to send a photograph. Route applicants to the matching NESP application page; the existing NESP application workflow owns the role-specific questions.
- Keep the copy neutral and inclusive. Do not use preferences such as "young," "recent graduate," gendered language, or any protected-trait requirement. Review any screening requirement for job-relatedness and accommodation handling.
- Use only NESP-owned or cleared images. The draft contains no personal data and no image upload is required.
- Keep pay, schedule, territory, equipment, travel, and paid-time language exactly to the listing data. The field listing states paid drive time both directions; the staff listing says paid drive time is handled in accordance with NESP policy; the freelance listing says travel and paid-time terms follow the approved contractor assignment terms for the work offered.

## Exact Manual Steps After Approval

These are the steps Craig would take manually. This worktree has performed none of them.

1. Open the official Craigslist Boston site and confirm the selected area is `Boston > Northwest/Merrimack`.
2. Choose `post to classifieds` / `new posting`.
3. Select `job offered`.
4. Select the approved category for the first role. Record the displayed fee before continuing.
5. Enter the approved title, posting description, employment type, compensation, and truthful location details from this document.
6. Enter the approved reply-to mailbox. Prefer Craigslist mail relay when appropriate. Do not use a personal mailbox unless Craig expressly approves it.
7. Add the approved map point if a map is required or useful. Confirm the pin does not disclose a private address or falsely suggest a single fixed worksite.
8. Leave images blank unless the exact image was approved. If adding one, upload only the cleared NESP-owned asset and verify that it contains no applicant or child data.
9. Continue to the confirmation page and review the unpublished preview line by line: site, subarea, category, fee, title, body, compensation, employment type, territory, application link, map, and image.
10. Stop if any field, fee, classification, or wording is wrong. Edit the draft before submission.
11. If the preview and total remain within the approved gate, proceed through Craigslist's required email confirmation, any displayed terms confirmation, and the payment step using Craig's approved account/payment method.
12. Complete the post only after Craigslist accepts the payment and any required phone verification. Do not use a VOIP or shared number for phone verification; Craigslist says a number can be associated with only one account at a time.
13. Save the Craigslist post URL, post ID, category, site, creation date, fee, and expiration date in the internal campaign record. Do not put applicant information in this repository.
14. Repeat steps 1-13 for the other approved roles. Each role is a separate post and each fee is a separate budget line.
15. Monitor the three posts manually. Paid posts remain live for 30 days; a repost is a new paid post and requires a fresh approval. Remove or edit a post when the role is filled or the copy is no longer accurate.
