# NESP Hiring Positioning

## Objective

Compete for photographers and photo-day staff against larger school and sports photography companies without attacking or making unsupported claims about any competitor.

Competitors named for internal planning include Lifetouch, Geskus Studios, and O'Connor Studios. Their names should not appear in public NESP job ads unless management specifically approves a factual comparison.

## NESP Advantages to Emphasize

### Local and family-run

NESP has served New England communities since 1975. Candidates should understand that they are joining a local operation where reliable people can be known, trusted, and given more responsibility.

### School and sports variety

The work includes youth leagues, teams, individual athletes, students, coaches, school staff, banners, and other picture-day assignments rather than a single narrow portrait format.

### Established assignments without running a full business

Photographers can focus on taking photographs and following the production workflow. NESP handles client acquisition, scheduling, products, printing, customer support, and fulfillment.

### In-house operation

NESP's in-house production and quality-control operation creates a direct connection between field performance and the final customer product.

### Seasonal return path

The ads should clearly state that dependable people can return each season and may advance into lead, staff, training, scheduling, or office-support responsibilities.

### Training for non-photographer roles

Table and assistant candidates should not be discouraged by a lack of professional camera experience. Reliability, communication, organization, and comfort working with children are the core requirements.

## Language to Avoid

- “Easy work”
- “Anyone can do it”
- Unapproved guarantees of hours
- Unapproved guarantees of year-round employment
- Claims that NESP pays more than a named competitor
- Negative comments about another company's quality, culture, ownership, or customers
- Promises of benefits that have not been confirmed
- Automatic advancement promises
- Language implying an AI system makes hiring decisions

## Strong Recruiting Messages

- “Recurring seasonal assignments with a local company”
- “Training and a clear photo-day system”
- “Opportunities to return and grow based on reliability and performance”
- “Work with schools, leagues, families, and community organizations”
- “Photographers focus on the assignment; NESP handles the production operation”
- “No photography experience required for assistant and table roles”
- “Weekday school assignments and weekend sports opportunities”

## Recommended Recruiting Channels

- Indeed
- Local Facebook community and job groups
- New England photography groups
- College photography, communications, education, and sports-management programs
- Community colleges and technical schools
- Youth sports coaches and league contacts
- Former NESP seasonal staff and employee referrals
- Local theater, dance, childcare, camp, and education networks for assistants/posers
- Hospitality, retail, and event-worker networks for table staff

## Application Funnel Message

Every advertisement should send applicants to one NESP application portal. The first question should ask which role or roles interest them. The system can then display the correct screening questions and work samples.
