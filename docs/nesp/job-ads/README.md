# NESP Seasonal Hiring Ads

These are review-ready draft listings for New England Sports Photo. They are not published automatically.

## Brand position

NESP should present itself as a local, family-run company that has served New England communities since 1975. The public job ads should focus on NESP's own strengths rather than naming or criticizing competitors:

- Family-run and locally trusted since 1975
- Youth sports picture days throughout Massachusetts, Rhode Island, New Hampshire, and Connecticut
- Professional picture-day systems and training
- In-house production and quality control
- Seasonal work with opportunities to return
- Real responsibility and advancement for dependable team members

## Canonical listings included

1. W-2 Seasonal Youth Sports Photographer - seasonal W-2 photographer role
2. Freelance/Contract Youth Sports Photographer - proposed contract assignments
3. Photography Assistant/Poser - seasonal picture-day role
4. On-Site Picture Day Table Staff - seasonal picture-day role
5. Part-Time Customer Service Representative - Methuen in-office W-2 customer service role

## Compensation and schedule assumptions to confirm before posting

These drafts use Craig-approved planning details for the hiring project:

- Freelance/contract photographer: $22-$27 per hour based on experience
- Photography assistant/poser: $20 per hour
- On-site picture day table staff: $20 per hour
- W-2 seasonal youth sports photographer: $22-$25 per hour based on experience
- Part-Time Customer Service Representative: $22-$25 per hour based on experience
- Work locations for field roles: Massachusetts, Rhode Island, New Hampshire, and Connecticut
- Primary fall season: September through November
- Expected field availability: weekdays after approximately 4:00 PM and weekends generally early morning through noon
- W-2 photographers use company-provided equipment
- Contractors must provide their own professional camera equipment, lenses, external flash, and backup equipment
- Paid time begins when the worker leaves home and continues through completion of the assignment
- Return travel is paid when the drive home exceeds one hour
- Background checks and reliable transportation are required for field roles

Craig must approve final classification, pay rate, payroll setup, overtime rules, travel policy, background-check process, and posting channel before any advertisement is published.

## Safety and hiring rules

- Do not publish any job ads from this repository without human approval.
- Do not automatically reject candidates using AI.
- Do not score protected characteristics or proxies such as age, name, photo, address, graduation year, disability, family status, race, ethnicity, religion, sex, or gender.
- AI may summarize job-related experience and flag missing information for human review.
- Independent-contractor classification must be reviewed for the actual work arrangement and state before the freelance role is posted.
- Applicant communications remain draft-only until a human approves the exact message.
